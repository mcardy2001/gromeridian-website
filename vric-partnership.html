Federally Funded Initiative

CAAIN Smart Farm Platform

Vineland and Homestead Growers are pioneering Canadian horticulture through collaborative Smart Farm innovation via the CAAIN (Canadian Agri-Food Automation and Intelligence Network) platform.

Vineland — Smart Farm Sandbox

  • Controlled pre-competitive environment for ERP and AI integration testing
  • Data governance and validation frameworks
  • Applied grower training programs
  • Research-grade data collection and analysis

Homestead — Commercial Living Lab

  • Real-world production environment for stress-testing validated solutions
  • Commercial conditions provide ground-truth feedback to researchers
  • Demonstration facility for prospective grower clients
  • Continuous feedback loop from field to lab

Research Findings

Phase 1 Learnings: Mprise & Homestead

Key findings from semi-structured interviews reveal critical success factors for horticulture BMP implementations.

System Readiness

Defined processes, standardized workflows, and documented SOPs must be in place before ERP configuration begins.

Data Readiness

Standardized and accessible data is the real prerequisite. Marketing talks about outputs, but data quality inputs are what matter.

Staff Readiness

Key-user buy-in, dedicated time freed from daily operations, and change management commitment are essential.

Key Insights

  • ERP implementation is an ongoing journey: Pre-Implementation, Implementation, and Post Go-live phases
  • Clear division of responsibilities: Provider owns methodology and config; Customer owns decisions and data quality
  • Upfront requirements analysis reduces risk with more precise scoping and fewer surprises
  • Digital readiness maturity: 30% laggards, 60% majority, 10% leaders — most growers need guided onboarding
